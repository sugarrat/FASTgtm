Primary Buyer Personas

Persona 3 BOOM Operator

  • Re-uses flow sheet for multiple clients
  • Needs flexible, multi-commodity capability
  • ROI-focused, 2-3 year payback requirement
  • Pain points: Equipment downtime = revenue loss, inflexible equipment
  • Decision criteria: Price (35%), reliability/uptime (30%), versatility (20%), support response time (15%)
Persona 3 BOOM Operator

Persona 1 Cost-Constrained Operations Manager

  • Mid-tier miner
  • Evaluated on cost per ton, uptime
  • Limited capital budget, needs board approval for > $100k
  • Pain points: budget, pressure to reduce opex, limited engineering staff
  • Decision criteria: Price (40%), lead time (25%), proven reliability (20%), support (15%)
Persona 1 Cost-Constrained Operations Manager

Persona 2 Brownfield Expansion Project Manager

  • Adding capacity to existing plant
  • Tight timeline and fixed budget
  • Doesn't want to over-engineer
  • Pain points: Equipment lead times delaying commissioning, budget overruns
  • Decision criteria: Delivery time (35%), price (30%), compatibility with existing equipment (20%), technical risk (15%)
Persona 2 Brownfield Expansion Project Manager

Buying Process Decision Influencers & Their Priorities

  • Process Engineer (Technical gate): Performance specifications, operating parameters
  • Operations Manager (User gate): Reliability, maintenance requirements, support
  • Procurement Manager (Commercial gate): Price, terms, delivery, risk management
  • Plant Manager (Budget holder): Total cost of ownership, business case
  • CFO/Board (Final approval >$X): Financial return, strategic fit
Buying Process Decision Influencers & Their Priorities

Buying Process Typical Journey

  1. Awareness (Problem recognition) - 0-2 months
    • Equipment failure, capacity expansion need, new project
  2. Consideration (Vendor shortlist) - 1-3 months
    • RFQ process, 2-3 vendors typically
  3. Evaluation (Technical & commercial) - 2-4 months
    • Technical specification review, site visits, reference checks
  4. Decision (Approvals & contracting) - 1-3 months
    • Multiple approval levels depending on value
  5. Implementation (Delivery & commissioning) - 3-6 months

  • Finance: Payment terms and financing options currently offered

Customer Tiers

Tier 1: Major Mining Corps (BHP, Rio, Vale, Glencore, etc.)

  • Strategy: Generally NOT the target for FAST spirals initially
  • Exception: Brownfield, non-critical applications where they specify "budget option acceptable"
  • Risk: Brand dilution if they associate your company with "cheap equipment"

Tier 2: Mid-Tier Miners ($500M-$5B revenue)

  • PRIMARY TARGET
  • Multiple sites, professional procurement, cost-conscious
  • Want brand reliability but don't need cutting-edge performance

Tier 3: Junior Miners & Contractors (<$500M revenue)

  • PRIMARY TARGET
  • Price-sensitive, limited technical resources
  • Need support but can't afford premium pricing

Tier 4: Backyard Operators

  • SECONDARY TARGET (Phase 2)
  • Highly price-sensitive, often purchasing through distributors
  • May need financing or rental options

Target Industries & Applications

Primary Markets (Years 1-2):

  • Mineral Sands: mitigate the risk of losing market share to low cost suppliers with this new product line.

Secondary Markets (Years 2-3):

  • Iron ore: North America, Western Australia
  • Chromite: Emerging market segment with less brand loyalty
  • Industrial minerals: silica sand, garnet

Applications to Avoid Initially:

  • Iron ore, because there is not an urgent need to protect market share
    • Challenge this approach - Brazilians setting up shop in Quebec to refurbish spirals.
  • Chromite, because the market is small
  • Coal, because this market is not growing and the spirals are large diameter.
